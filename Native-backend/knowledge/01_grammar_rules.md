# English Grammar Rules for Spanish Speakers
## NATIVE 2.0 — Pedagogical Knowledge Base

---

## 1. Verb Tenses — Most Common Errors

### Present Simple vs Present Continuous
Spanish speakers often confuse these two tenses.

**Rule:** Use Present Simple for habits and facts. Use Present Continuous for actions happening right now.

| Error (Spanish influence) | Correct English |
|--------------------------|-----------------|
| I am going to school every day. | I go to school every day. |
| She is working here since 2020. | She has worked here since 2020. |
| He is knowing the answer. | He knows the answer. |

**Key insight:** Stative verbs (know, believe, understand, want, love, hate, need) NEVER use continuous form.

---

### Past Simple vs Present Perfect
This is one of the most frequent errors for Spanish speakers because Spanish uses "pretérito perfecto" differently.

**Rule:** Use Past Simple for completed actions at a specific time. Use Present Perfect for actions connected to the present.

| Error | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| I have seen him yesterday. | I saw him yesterday. |
| Did you ever visit London? | Have you ever visited London? |
| She has finished the project last week. | She finished the project last week. |

---

### Future Forms
Spanish speakers often overuse "will" when "going to" or Present Continuous is more natural.

**Rule:**
- Will → spontaneous decisions, predictions without evidence
- Going to → planned intentions, predictions with evidence
- Present Continuous → fixed arrangements

| Context | Correct Form |
|---------|-------------|
| Spontaneous decision | I'll have the soup. |
| Prior plan | I'm going to study tonight. |
| Fixed appointment | I'm meeting her at 3pm. |

---

## 2. Subject Pronouns — Never Drop Them

In Spanish, subject pronouns are often omitted because verb endings indicate the subject.
In English, subject pronouns are ALWAYS required.

| Error | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| Is raining. | It is raining. |
| Was a good movie. | It was a good movie. |
| Is important to study. | It is important to study. |

---

## 3. Agreement Errors

### Subject-Verb Agreement
**Rule:** Third person singular (he/she/it) always requires -s in Present Simple.

| Error | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| She go to work every day. | She goes to work every day. |
| He don't understand. | He doesn't understand. |
| The team are working hard. | The team is working hard. |

### Adjective Agreement
In Spanish, adjectives agree in gender and number. In English, adjectives never change.

| Error | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| She is a beautifuls woman. | She is a beautiful woman. |
| They are very intelligents. | They are very intelligent. |

---

## 4. Articles — A Common Source of Errors

### Definite Article (The)
Spanish speakers often add "the" where it's not needed, or omit it where it is.

| Error | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| The life is beautiful. | Life is beautiful. |
| The love is complicated. | Love is complicated. |
| I go to the school every day. | I go to school every day. |
| She is the doctor. | She is a doctor. |

### Indefinite Article (A/An)
Use "an" before vowel sounds (not just vowel letters).

| Error | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| She is a engineer. | She is an engineer. |
| He is a honest man. | He is an honest man. |
| It was a unique experience. | It was a unique experience. ✓ (u sounds like "you") |

---

## 5. Prepositions — False Friends

Spanish prepositions rarely translate directly to English.

| Spanish | Wrong English | Correct English |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| en casa | in home | at home |
| depender de | depend of | depend on |
| casarse con | marry with | marry (no preposition) |
| soñar con | dream of | dream about/of |
| pensar en | think in | think about/of |
| interesarse en | be interested in | be interested in ✓ |

---

## 6. Double Negatives

In Spanish, double negatives are grammatically correct. In English, they create a positive meaning.

| Spanish logic (incorrect in English) | Correct English |
|--------------------------------------|-----------------|
| I don't know nothing. | I don't know anything. |
| She didn't say nothing. | She didn't say anything. |
| There isn't nobody here. | There isn't anybody here. |

---

## 7. Word Order

In Spanish, adjectives often follow nouns. In English, adjectives come before nouns.

| Spanish order (incorrect) | Correct English |
|--------------------------|-----------------|
| A car red | A red car |
| The house big | The big house |

Questions follow Subject-Auxiliary-Verb order in English:
| Error | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| You are coming? | Are you coming? |
| He likes pizza? | Does he like pizza? |

---

## 8. Modal Verbs

Modal verbs (can, could, should, would, must, may, might) are always followed by the base form of the verb — never with "to" (except "ought to").

| Error | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| She can to swim. | She can swim. |
| You should to study. | You should study. |
| He must to leave now. | He must leave now. |

---

## Pedagogical Note for NATIVE Agent

When a Spanish speaker makes one of these errors, the correction should be integrated naturally into the conversation response — never as a grammar lesson. The character should echo the correct form naturally, then continue the conversation. Only highlight the error explicitly if it has been repeated 3 or more times in the same session.
